Exploring the Hidden Gems of Tuscany

March 17, 2025

Tuscany is more than just Florence and Siena. Last month, I wandered through the rolling hills of Val d’Orcia, where medieval villages like Pienza and Montalcino steal your breath with their charm. The golden light at sunset, the taste of pecorino cheese paired with a glass of Brunello wine—it’s a sensory journey worth taking.

Beyond the well-trodden paths, there’s so much to uncover. Start in Pienza, a UNESCO World Heritage site known as the "ideal Renaissance city." Its narrow streets are lined with artisan shops selling local cheeses and olive oils. A short ride away, Montalcino offers not just world-class wine but also panoramic views from its 14th-century fortress. For a quieter escape, Bagno Vignoni’s thermal springs are a hidden delight—perfect for a soak after a day of exploring.

My tip? Skip the overcrowded tourist traps and rent a Vespa. The freedom to wind through olive groves and vineyards, stopping at tiny trattorias for homemade pici pasta, is unmatched. Tuscany’s magic lies in these small, unscripted moments.
